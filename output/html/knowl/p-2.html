Recall that a complex \(n \times n\) matrix \(A\) is called normal if \(A A\ad = A\ad A\) where \(\ast\) represents the conjugate transpose. The spectral theorem for normal matrices says that \(A\) can be diagonalized as

\begin{equation*} A = U D U\ad \end{equation*}

where \(D\) is a diagonal matrix of eigenvalues of \(A\) and \(U\) is a unitary matrix of associated eigenvectors.
