Let \(T\) be a compact self-adjoint operator on a Hilbert space \(\hilbert\text{.}\) Then \(T\) has only countable distinct eigenvalues. Enumerating the eigenvalues as \(\{\la_1, \la_2, \ldots\}\text{,}\) let \(P_n\) denote the projection of \(\hilbert\) onto \(\ker(T - \la_n)\text{.}\) Then \(P_nP_m = P_m P_n = 0\) when \(n \neq m\text{,}\) each \(\la_n\) is real, and

\begin{equation*} T = \sum_{n=1}^\infty \la_n P_n, \end{equation*}

where the sum converges in the norm of \(\BH\text{.}\)
