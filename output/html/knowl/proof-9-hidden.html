Suppose that \(T\) is a compact self-adjoint operator on a Hilbert space \(\hilbert\text{.}\)

By Proposition 1.3.4 and Lemma 1.3.5, there exists a real number \(\la_1\) in \(\sigma_p(T)\) with \(\abs{\la_1} = \norm{T}\text{.}\) Let \(E_1 = \ker(T - \la_1)\) and \(P_1:\hilbert \to E_1\) be the projection onto \(E_1\text{.}\) Denote by \(\hilbert_2\) the orthogonal complement of \(E_1\) in \(\hilbert\text{.}\) By Proposition 1.3.2, \(E_1\) reduces \(T\text{,}\) and so \(\hilbert_2\) reduces \(T\text{.}\) Then define \(T_2\) to be the restriction of \(T\) to \(\hilbert_2\text{;}\) that is \(T_2 = T_{\hilbert_2}\text{.}\)
