With this language, diagonalization becomes the decomposition of operators into sums of projections. That is, given an operator \(A\) on a (complex) finite dimensional Hilbert space \(\hilbert\text{,}\) denote by \((\la_i, E_i)\) the \(i\)th eigenvalue and corresponding eigenspace. Recall that each eigenspace is \(E_i = \ker(A - \la_i)\text{.}\) A normal matrix will always have \(\hilbert = \bigoplus_{i=1}^k E_i\) and we can write

\begin{equation} A = \sum_{i=1}^k \la_i P_{i}.\tag{1.1.1} \end{equation}

where \(P_i\) is the projection operator onto \(\ker(A - \la_i)\text{.}\)
