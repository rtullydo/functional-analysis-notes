As in finite dimensions, the range of a projection can be used to decompose a space. Let \(P\) be a projection with \(\ran P = \M\) and \(\ker P = \N\text{.}\) Kernel and range are closed subspaces, and thus Hilbert spaces in their own right. Then construct the space \(\M \oplus \N\text{.}\) It is straighforward that \(\M \oplus \N\) is isomorphic to \(\hilbert\text{,}\) and in the usual abuse of notation, we'll just say \(\hilbert = \M \oplus \N\text{,}\) or that \(\hilbert\) has been orthogonally decomposed.
