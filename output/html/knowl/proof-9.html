Proof of Theorem 1.4.1..

Suppose that \(T\) is a compact self-adjoint operator on a Hilbert space \(\hilbert\text{.}\)

By Proposition 1.4.4 and Lemma 1.4.5, there exists a real number \(\la_1\) in \(\sigma_p(T)\) with \(\abs{\la_1} = \norm{T}\text{.}\) Let \(E_1 = \ker(T - \la_1)\) and \(P_1:\hilbert \to E_1\) be the projection onto \(E_1\text{.}\) Denote by \(\hilbert_2\) the orthogonal complement of \(E_1\) in \(\hilbert\text{.}\) By Proposition 1.4.2, \(E_1\) reduces \(T\text{,}\) and so \(\hilbert_2\) reduces \(T\text{.}\) Then define \(T_2\) to be the restriction of \(T\) to \(\hilbert_2\text{;}\) that is \(T_2 = T\vert_{\hilbert_2} = T P_1\text{.}\) Using block operator representation of \(T\) on \(\hilbert = E_1 \oplus \hilbert_2\text{,}\) it is easy to see that \(T_2\) is a compact self-adjoint operator on \(\hilbert_2\text{.}\)

If \(T_2 = 0\text{,}\) we're done. If not, we can repeat the process to produce an eigenvalue \(\la_2\) and associated eigenspace \(E_2\text{.}\) Continiuing, we either terminate with some \(T_n = 0\text{,}\) or we proceed by induction to produce an infinite sequence \(\la_n\text{.}\) In either case, we end up with a sequence of eigenvalues ordered by magnitude and pairwise orthogonal eigenspaces.

The last piece to be shown is that the accumulation point of the eigenvalues is precisely 0. The monotone bounded sequence theorem gives a real number \(\alpha\) so that \(\abs{\la_n} \to \alpha.\) We will argue that \(\alpha = 0\text{.}\)
