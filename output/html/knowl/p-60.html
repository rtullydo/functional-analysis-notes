Let \(h \in \ker(A - \la)\) and \(g \in \ker(A - \mu)\text{.}\) By PropositionÂ 1.4.2, we have that \(A\ad g = \cc\mu g\text{.}\) Then

\begin{align*} \la\ip{h}{g} \amp=\ip{A h}{g}\\ \amp= \ip{h}{A\ad g}\\ \amp= \ip{h}{\cc\mu g}\\ \amp= \mu\ip{h}{g}. \end{align*}

Thus, \((\la - \mu)\ip{h}{g} = 0\text{.}\) Since \(\la, \mu\) are distinct, we must have \(h\perp g\) and thus that \(\ker(A - \la) \perp \ker(A - \mu)\text{.}\)
